Appenzeller Sennenhund

Characteristics
Size: Medium
Coat: Double coat
Height: 50-56cm (20-22in)
Colour: Tricolour, black or Havana brown with white and reddish-brown marking
Life span: 12-14 years
Pros Cons
Good With Other Dogs Not Affectionate With Family
Alert with stranger Need to spend many time daily for exercise
Easy to train Loud barking

Description

The Appenzeller Sennenhund is a Swiss breed of medium-sized working dog. It originates in the Appenzell region of north-eastern Switzerland, and is one of four regional breeds of Sennenhund or Swiss mountain dog, all of which are characterised by a distinctive tricolour coat.

Appearance

The Appenzeller Sennenhund Dog has a distinct look and is black or brown with very symmetrical markings. They must have white and rust markings with the rust being between the white and the black or brown. They have a double coat with the topcoat being tight, thick and shiny. Their undercoat is also thick but can be black, brown or grey. Their ears are small and triangular and hang down to the dogâ€™s cheeks. They should have small, alert eyes and a curled tail .

Health

The average life-span for Appenzeller Sennenhund is 12-14 years. The Appenzeller is closely related to three other Swiss mountain dogs: the Entlebucher, the Bernese and the Greater Swiss, all of whom have issues with hip and elbow dysplasia, hereditary eye diseases, and bloat. So you can expect those health issues in the Appenzeller.

Should I buy this breed?

Appenzeller Sennenhund known for being a versatile working and family dog who's smart, cheerful, self-assured, reliable, and fearless. His slight wariness around strangers and tendency to bark makes him a good watchdog, but he needs lots of early socialization so he doesn't become overly suspicious. This breed also required someone who is active so they can get a lot of exercise to prevent destructive behaviour. This breed is not ideal for first-time owner because this breed is strong-willed mind of his own, requiring a confident owner who can take charge.
