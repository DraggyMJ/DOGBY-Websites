Rhodesian Ridgeback

Characteristics
Height: 67 - 74 cm (26-29 in) [male]
61 - 66 cm (24-26 in) [female]
Coat: Short and dense, sleek and glossy in appearance
Color: Light wheaten to red wheaten. A little white on the chest and toes is permissible. A dark muzzle and ears permissible. Excessive black hair throughout the coat is undesirable. Two nose colors are permissible, black and liver.
Life span: 10-13 years
Pros Cons
Intelligent and Brave Destructiveness when bored or not exercised enough
Hunting and Guarding Dog Vigorous exercise requirements
Calm and Quiet Required Experienced Owner to Maintain

Description

The Rhodesian Ridgeback is a large scenthound and sighthound dog breed bred in the Southern Africa region. Its forebears can be traced to the semi-domesticated ridged hunting and guardian dogs of the Khoikhoi. These were interbred with European dogs by the early colonists of the Cape Colony of southern Africa. The original breed standard was drafted by F. R. Barnes, in Bulawayo, Southern Rhodesia (now Zimbabwe), in 1922, and approved by the South African Kennel Union in 1927. The Rhodesian Ridgeback at present is the only registered breed indigenous to Southern Africa.

Appearance

They come in only one color, wheaten, which spans every shade seen in a wheat field, from pale flaxen to the burnished red of a maturing crop. Ridgebacks also have two nose colors: black and the less commonly seen brown. Handsome, strong, muscular and active dog, symmetrical in outline, capable of great endurance with fair amount of speed.

Health

The Rhodesian Ridgeback dog, which has an average lifespan of 10 to 13 years, does not suffer from any serious health conditions. However, it may be prone to minor issues such as elbow dysplasia, canine hip dysplasia (CHD), and hypothyroidism. Deafness and dermoid sinus are also occasionally seen in the breed.
