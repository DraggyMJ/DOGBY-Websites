Quality Dog Foods and Treats

Different dog breed have their own nutritional needs. It is advis toe research whats your dog nutritional needs. The best way to feed the dogs or puppies is feeding with a combination of wet dog food and dry dog food in addition to dental care and treats products confers the widest range of benefits. Dogs gain the widest range of health benefits through a diet of dry food, wet food, dental care and treats.

Wet food

Wet food starts with grinding the protein sources, or meat ingredients. Then a gravy that contains vitamins, minerals, and grains is added. Once those are mixed, the food is cooked and sterilized to produce the canned product.

Benefits

  • Wide Range of Flavors
  • Higher Moisture Content
  • Easier to Chew

Dry food

Dry food contains very similar ingredients, but instead of adding gravy and canning the product, the meat mixture is pulverized in order to create a consistent dough that can be cooked. Once cooked, the dough is extruded through specifically shaped holes to form kibble. The kibble undergoes a drying process, and then is sprayed with fats, oils, vitamins, and minerals and packaged before the fats and oils can spoil.

Benefits

  • Reduce Dental Plaque
  • Healthier Gums
  • Easier to Store

Treats for Dog

Pet treats are dietary supplements given to cats and dogs just like we eat snacks. Pets especially dogs get bored very easily and quickly and to keep them active, engaged and get their attention, owner needs to give them dog treats.

Benefits

  • Be used a part of training and as a positive reward for good behaviour
  • Relieve boredom and reduce destructive behaviour
  • Dental treats and gnaws can promote healthy teeth and provide oral care

Training Your Dog

Training is an essential part of owning a dog and can be started at any age. Training builds confidence, provides mental stimulation and strengthens the human-animal bond. Before you get started, make a list of basic commands you want him to know: "sit”, "stay”, "come”, "down” or "no”. You might also want to control his barking, teach him not to beg for food and how to avoid accidents in the house. These are all very doable and all it requires is consistency, praise, occasional treats and lots of patience and positivity.
